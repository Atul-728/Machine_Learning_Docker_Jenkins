About Our Milk Quality Grader

Leveraging Machine Learning for Precision Dairy Quality Assessment

Project Overview

The Milk Quality Grader is an advanced machine learning system designed to accurately assess and grade milk quality based on multiple parameters. Our system provides dairy farmers, processors, and quality control specialists with a reliable, objective method for evaluating milk samples.

Using parameters such as pH, temperature, taste, odor, fat content, turbidity, and color, our algorithm can predict milk quality with over 95% accuracy, sorting samples into low, medium, and high-quality grades.

Our Methodology

Data Collection

We collected and analyzed data from thousands of milk samples with various quality parameters.

Data Cleaning

Rigorous pre-processing to ensure data quality by handling missing values and removing duplicates.

Feature Engineering

Transformation and selection of the most relevant features for accurate prediction.

Model Training

Training and evaluation of multiple ML algorithms to find the best performer.

Model Comparison

We evaluated multiple machine learning models including Logistic Regression, Decision Tree, Random Forest, KNN, and Gaussian Naive Bayes to identify the most accurate approach for milk quality prediction.

After rigorous testing, the Random Forest classifier emerged as the top performer with over 95% accuracy on test data.

Quality Parameters

pH Level

Measure of acidity, with optimal milk typically having pH between 6.5-6.7.

Temperature

Affects bacterial growth and overall milk quality.

Taste

Sensory evaluation of flavor profile.

Odor

Smell characteristics that indicate freshness.

Fat Content

Percentage of milk fat, affecting richness and nutritional value.

Turbidity

Measure of clarity or cloudiness in the milk.

Color

Visual appearance indicating quality and composition.

Key Findings

  • Strong correlation between pH and Temperature (r = 0.72)
  • Fat content significantly influences taste perception
  • Odor and turbidity are closely related quality indicators
  • Random Forest algorithm achieved the highest prediction accuracy
  • Combined parameter analysis provides more reliable grading than any single metric

Our research demonstrates that a multi-parameter approach to milk quality assessment outperforms traditional single-factor evaluations.
